    Currency Symbol * (e.g. FRW) help The currency symbol for money in your country e.g. $, FRW

    Sales Estimates
    Average number of customers using service per day * help Average sales units sold in a day

    Average customer spend on service each time * (Money Value) help Average sale price of a unit.

    Average service sales per day * (Calculated) help Average sales value in a day

    Sales in Year 1 * (Calculated) help Sales (money value) in the first year of the plan.

    Sales Change % (each year of growth or decline) * help Percentage that the sales are expected to grow each year. e.g.
    10 percent growth will make 1000 worth 1100 in year two and 1464 in year five.
    50 percent growth will make 1000 worth 1500 in year two and 5062 in year five.

    Cost of Sales % * help Cost of the goods and services sold as a percentage of the sales. If you sell $1000 worth of goods or services and they cost you $400 (to buy, provide or manufacture) then you have a 40% cost of sales.


    Expenses Estimates Year 1
    Bank Charges & Interest * (Money Value) help Bank charges and interest

    Depreciation * (Money Value) help Depreciation is a way of charging for a fixed asset (e.g. equipment) over its life. For example, if you buy a piece of equipment for 1000 and it has a life of 4 years, then you would charge 250 here in the income statement.

    Wages & Employment * (Money Value) help Employee wages, plus any on costs like national insurance.

    Insurances * (Money Value) help Costs of building, employment and other insurances

    Advertising & Marketing * (Money Value) help Cost of advertising and other promotions.

    Office Costs (e.g. ink) * (Money Value) help Cost of consumables like ink, pens and other office items.

    Other * (Money Value) help Any expenses not covered by the other categories.

    Rent & Lease * (Money Value) help Cost of rent, leases and rates on premises.

    Professional Fees * (Money Value) help Cost of accountants, lawyers and other consultancy services.

    Postage & Shipping * (Money Value) help Cost of postage, couriers and other shipping.

    Travel * (Money Value) help Cost of work related travel (e.g. visiting customers)

    Utilities, Gas, Electricity & Internet * (Money Value) help Cost of heat, light, telecoms and internet.

    Total Expenses in Year 1 * (Calculated) help Expenses of running the business in the first year of the plan. These are overhead costs not linked to the goods and services sold e.g. telephone, building insurance, administration staff, light and heat, professional fees

    Expenses Growth % each year * help Percentage that the overhead expenses are expected to grow each year. When deciding on the percentage figure consider inflation and also how your five year objective will affect the size of the business and its costs e.g.
    2 percent growth in 1000 of expenses will make 1020 in year two and 1082 in year five.


    Cash Estimates
    Days credit given to customers * help The number of days you give your customers to pay for goods or services provided e.g.
    Business to business: 30 days is often the formally stated credit in many industries, but it can take longer in real life. Also some businesses that are new or have a poor credit history may be asked for so called \'cash up front\', that is 0 days credit.
    Retail: customers are rarely given credit, so likely to be 0 days credit.

    Days credit given by suppliers * help The number of days you get from your suppliers to pay for the goods or services provided. 30 days is often the formally stated credit in many industries, but it can be longer in real life. However, if you are starting a new business without a credit history then you may not be able to get any credit and could be asked to pay \'cash up front\', that is 0 days credit.

    Cash invested by owners * help This is money (cash or bank deposits) put into the business for the long term by the owner(s) during the first year. This figure is used as part of the cash flow forecast so it is important to only include actual money and not equivalent investments, like giving the business equipment or some other asset.
